Join Us


Become a Friend of the Library and join a great group of people who are enthusiastic about books, libraries, reading, and literacy.

Print and fill out this form and mail it with your check to the address on the form. Pre-printed membership forms are available at the Loma Colorado Friends Store.

You can also drop your form and pay by cash, card or check at the Loma Colorado Friends' Store.

  • Friends receive an additional 10% discount off already low Friends Store prices at the Loma Colorado Library by presenting their current Friends membership card at checkout.
  • Friends who are 55 or older may be eligible to participate in the Sandoval County Retired Senior Volunteer Program (RSVP). Volunteers accepted into RSVP may receive some compensation.
  • All Friends are invited to the Holiday Party Luncheon each December and the Annual Meeting & Volunteer Recognition Luncheon each April.
  • Friends receive periodic Newsletters and, if they provide an email address, brief monthly email updates about Friends' activities.

Books for Babies


By reading to babies and toddlers, parents help their children develop language skills that start them on the path to success in reading and learning. The Friends and national literacy program Books for Babies help new parents with this role.

New parents at Presbyterian Rust Medical Center receive a Books for Babies kit from the Friends that includes a board book for baby, a certificate for baby’s first library card, and brochures with reading tips and early literacy information from nationally-recognized educational organizations. The kits are available in both English and Spanish.

Loma Colorado Library’s Teen Advisory Group (TAG) assembles the kits at Friends-sponsored "pizza and packing" parties.

Sales of the Friends' Tote bags help fund the Books for Babies program. These sturdy bags are available at the monthly book sale, at the front desk in each library and in the Friends' Store at the Loma Colorado Main Library.

Awards & Grants


PNM Power-Up Grant

The Friends were awarded a PNM Resources Foundation “Power Up” grant of $19,995 to design, purchase, and install a shade sail over the Loma Colorado Main Library's concrete back patio area.

The sail transformed the area into a shaded outdoor classroom that benefits all Rio Rancho residents.

Brindle Fund Grant

For the second year in a row the Brindle Fund Santa Fe Community Foundation awarded a grant for our Books for Babies program. The Brindle Fund is a small, family foundation that focuses on early childhood and children’s literacy.

The grant moves the Friends closer to the goal of expanding our Books for Babies program to another local hospital.

Currently, the Friends supply Books for Babies backpacks to all new mothers at Presbyterian Rust Hospital in Rio Rancho, the Sandoval County WIC program, and Loma Colorado Library's Baby Bumblebee program.
